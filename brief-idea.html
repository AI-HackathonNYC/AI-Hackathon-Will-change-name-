How It Works

LLM agents read the reports.
Weather data enters the graph.

An agentic LLM pipeline extracts counterparty data from attestation PDFs, builds a geographic knowledge graph, and injects weather signals as stress inputs.

Attestation Extraction

LLM reads Circle, Tether, Paxos PDFs. Extracts banks, percentages, asset classes, maturity profiles, jurisdictions.

Circle · Tether · Paxos reports

Geographic Knowledge Graph

Stablecoin → Bank → City/State → Asset Class. Geographic anchoring makes weather data actionable.

Weather Signal Injection ★

NOAA forecasts and NHC tracks enter the graph as stress modifiers on geographic nodes. A storm in Miami stresses Miami bank nodes.

Counterparty Monitoring

Named banks tracked against news, FDIC watch lists, CDS spreads. Weather stress compounds with financial stress.

Demo Script — Five Beats

From dashboard overview to the close in under two minutes.

1

Show the dashboard. Six stablecoins scored across six dimensions. Map shows custodian bank locations. "We built this by having an LLM read every major attestation report and map the counterparties geographically."

2

Drop a hurricane on the map. Select "Category 4, SE Florida." Storm cone appears. Banks in the zone highlight. Two stablecoins shift scores as stress propagates through the graph.

3

Click into the explanation. LLM generates: "USDC counterparty risk elevated. 12% of reserves at institutions in SE Florida. $4.2B insurance liability in Miami-Dade increases deposit stress."

4

Switch to SVB backtest. "Rewind to March 2023." Katabatic's score flags risk 48 hours before the depeg. "8.3% of reserves at SVB, showing elevated deposit outflow signals."

5

Close the pitch. "Monthly audits told you USDC was fine. Katabatic would have told you to move your money. The risk isn't on the blockchain — it's in the weather, the banks, the geography."
